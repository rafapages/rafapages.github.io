The iPad dashboard that's there when you are.

A full-screen kiosk browser for iPad. Point it at your smart-home dashboard — or any website — and it shows up when someone walks in, then rests when the room's empty. Everything runs on-device.

The idea

Other kiosk apps are built for shops and museums — heavy, pricey, always on. DashPad is the opposite: a small, focused app that mounts an iPad on your wall, shows the page you care about, and gets out of the way.

Present when you are. Idle when you're not.

DashPad uses the front camera to notice when someone's in the room — but not the way you'd expect. No video stream, no recording, nothing ever leaves the iPad.

01

A quick glance

Every few seconds the camera wakes for about three seconds, captures one frame, and turns off again.

02

On-device check

That frame is analyzed right on the iPad for room brightness and whether a person is in view — whole-body or face, your choice.

03

Show or rest

Someone there? The dashboard stays up. Empty room? It counts down and switches to an idle screen. Dark room? It skips the camera entirely.

04

The frame is gone

Processed in memory and immediately discarded. Never written to disk, never logged, never sent anywhere.

Privacy

It isn't a setting. It's the architecture.

DashPad is built so your camera data physically can't go anywhere — not because we promise, but because there's nowhere for it to go.

on-device · in-memory · never stored

01

Nothing is stored.

Frames are analyzed in memory and discarded the instant the check is done.

02

Nothing is transmitted.

The app makes no outbound requests of its own. Only the dashboard you point it at touches the network.

03

Nothing is tracked.

No analytics, no crash SDKs, no telemetry, no third-party frameworks of any kind.

04

Nothing is hidden.

The full source is public — anyone can verify exactly what the camera pipeline does.

Smart idle screens.

A clean clock, solid black to save power, or any page as a screensaver.

Locks to the app.
