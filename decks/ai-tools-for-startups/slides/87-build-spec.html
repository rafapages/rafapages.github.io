A good spec is just RICCE for software

Same muscle you built for prompting: be explicit up front. Draft it with Claude. Describe the idea, let it ask you questions, and refine until the doc is unambiguous.

What a spec should pin down

  • What it does: the one job, in a sentence.
  • Who uses it & the screens they see.
  • The data: what's stored, what's shown.
  • Look & feel: style, references, tone.
  • Out of scope: what NOT to build yet.
interview-tracker.md
# Customer Interview Tracker
Goal: log & tag user interviews; spot themes.
## Screens
1. List of interviews (date, person, tags)
2. Detail view with notes + key quotes
## Data
interview: name, company, date, notes, tags[]
## Style
clean, light, one accent colour. No login v1.
## Not now
auth, export, AI summaries

Keep the spec as the source of truth. When you want a change, change the doc, not a pile of follow-up chat messages. It's how the build stays coherent.
