Reasoning models:
let it "think"

A reasoning model is the same machine given room to think out loud first — intermediate steps become context that steers a better final answer.

Q: A startup burns $40k/mo with $260k in the bank. How many months of runway?
Standard LLM — answers immediately
"About 5 months."
blurts the most plausible-sounding number
Reasoning model — thinks first
Step 1. Runway = cash ÷ monthly burn.
Step 2. Cash = $260k, burn = $40k/mo.
Step 3. 260 ÷ 40 = 6.5.
"6.5 months of runway."

Far better at math, logic, and multi-step planning; but slower and more expensive (you pay for thinking tokens).
Reduces errors without eliminating them.
